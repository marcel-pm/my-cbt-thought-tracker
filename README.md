# Thought Tracker

A simple, private CBT-based thought tracking app that runs entirely in your browser. No account needed, no data sent anywhere. Everything you log stays on your device.

Built for anyone working through anxiety, burnout, or negative thinking patterns — particularly people doing CBT (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy) who want a quick way to log and challenge their thoughts throughout the day without carrying paper worksheets around.

---

## How your data is handled

**Your entries never leave your phone.** The app stores everything in your browser's local storage — the same mechanism used by banking apps for session data. No server ever receives your thoughts. Anyone who finds this URL sees a blank app with zero data in it.

---

## What the app includes

### Thought log (Stage 1)
The starting point. When you notice a shift in how you feel, you record:
- The situation (where you were, who you were with, what was happening)
- The emotion and how intense it felt on a scale of 0-100%
- Any physical sensations (tight chest, butterflies, etc.)
- The automatic thought that went through your mind, and how much you believed it

The goal is to catch thoughts quickly before they fade.

### Perspective-taking (Exercise 1)
A guided exercise that helps you look at a situation that upset you from different angles. You record your initial interpretation, then work through a set of prompts designed to surface alternative viewpoints — what a trusted friend might say, whether it will matter in 10 years, how you would respond with no fear. You finish by reflecting on how your feelings shifted.

### Thought on trial (Exercise 2)
An imaginary court case for your negative thought. You play defense (what evidence supports the thought?), prosecution (what evidence contradicts it?), and judge (what is the fairest, most balanced verdict?). This is one of the core techniques in CBT for testing whether a thought is actually accurate rather than just believable.

### Reference guide
A full in-app guide covering how CBT works, the three most common thinking biases (catastrophizing, excluding the positive, and personalization), and step-by-step instructions for all exercises. No need to go back to a PDF or worksheet.

## Getting it on your iPhone home screen

This app is designed to work like a native app when added to your home screen on iOS.

1. Open the app URL in **Safari** on your iPhone (it must be Safari, not Chrome or Firefox)
2. Tap the **Share** icon at the bottom of the screen (the box with an arrow pointing up)
3. Scroll down and tap **Add to Home Screen**
4. Give it a name and tap **Add**

The app will now appear on your home screen and open fullscreen with no browser address bar, just like a native app.

---

## Daily reminders

The app can send you push notifications throughout the day to remind you to log a thought.

1. Open the app and go to the **Settings** tab
2. Select the times you want to be nudged (morning, midday, afternoon, evening)
3. Tap **Enable reminders** and grant notification permission when prompted

Note: notifications only work when the app is added to your home screen and opened from there, not from a browser tab.

---

## Exporting your entries

All export options are in the **Settings** tab.

- **Export as plain text** — a readable, formatted summary of all your entries
- **Export as JSON** — structured data, useful if you want to share entries with a therapist or import them elsewhere
- **Share to iCloud Drive / Files app** — triggers the iOS share sheet so you can save directly to iCloud Drive, AirDrop to another device, or send via email

---

## Clearing your data

Settings also includes a **Delete all entries** option. This permanently removes everything from local storage on your device. You will be asked to confirm before anything is deleted. Export first if you want to keep a copy.

## Background: what is CBT?

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy is a well-researched approach to managing anxiety, depression, low self-esteem, and other mental health challenges. The core idea is that it is not events themselves that cause emotional distress, but the way we interpret them. Our thinking can be biased in ways we are not always aware of, and those biases affect how we feel and behave.

CBT gives you practical tools to catch those automatic thoughts, examine whether they are accurate, and replace distorted interpretations with more balanced ones. This app puts those tools in your pocket.

This app is based on the Psychology Tools guide "Challenging Your Negative Thinking" and the worksheets that accompany it.
